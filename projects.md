# 1. Ibiza

URL: https://ibiza.rotsi.co.ke

## Clean Project Description

Ibiza is a merchant operating system for African SMEs. It brings CRM, sales tracking, inventory, storefronts, payments, WhatsApp communication, reports, and automations into one dashboard.

The platform is designed for business owners who are already selling but are losing time and money because their operations are spread across WhatsApp chats, Instagram DMs, Excel sheets, notebooks, M-Pesa records, stock books, staff updates, and memory.

Ibiza is not just a customer list or a basic accounting tool. It helps merchants see what is happening in their business, act quickly, and reduce repetitive manual work.

## Client-Facing Pitch

Ibiza gives a business owner one place to manage customers, sales, inventory, payments, reports, online orders, and customer communication.

Instead of switching between WhatsApp, Instagram, Excel, M-Pesa records, stock books, and separate staff updates, the merchant can open one dashboard and quickly understand:

- who bought what
- which clients need follow-up
- what stock is moving or running out
- which sales are coming from POS, storefront, or WhatsApp
- which expenses are affecting profit
- which customers are ready for rebooking, renewal, or repeat purchase
- which marketing or communication tasks can be automated

The value is simple: less manual follow-up, fewer missed sales, better customer retention, and clearer business decisions.

## Best-Fit Clients

Ibiza is best for growing businesses that already have customers and daily activity, but whose operations are becoming hard to manage manually.

Good-fit clients include:

- boutiques, cosmetic shops, electronics shops, food vendors, hardware stores, and other product-based retailers
- salons, barbershops, clinics, dentists, spas, gyms, coaches, photographers, agencies, and other service businesses
- Instagram and WhatsApp sellers who receive inquiries manually
- businesses using Excel for stock, sales, or client records
- SMEs with staff who need controlled access to sales, stock, clients, and corrections
- businesses that want a simple storefront without building a full ecommerce website
- merchants who want WhatsApp broadcasts, customer follow-ups, receipts, and payment flows in one system

The ideal customer is not necessarily technical. It is the owner who says:

> I am getting orders, but I am losing track of customers, stock, payments, and follow-ups.

That is the core problem Ibiza is built to solve.

## Product-Based Business Value

For product merchants, Ibiza works like a control center for selling physical goods. The business can manage products, stock, sales, POS activity, suppliers, expenses, public storefronts, checkout links, receipts, and reports.

A boutique, for example, can upload products, publish a mobile-friendly shop, receive orders, track stock reduction, view top-selling items, and follow up with customers through WhatsApp. The system turns scattered selling into a repeatable sales process.

## Service-Based Business Value

For service businesses, Ibiza supports customer relationships and repeat revenue. A barber, dentist, salon, coach, photographer, or clinic does not only need sales records. They need to know which clients are booked, active, overdue, retained, or ready for renewal.

Useful views include:

- client pipeline
- renewals
- customer relationship history
- service analytics
- rebooking and retention signals

This makes Ibiza strong for businesses where repeat visits matter more than one-off transactions.

## Technical Description

Ibiza is a modern web application built with React, TypeScript, Vite, Tailwind CSS, React Query, Firebase, Socket.IO, and a modular API structure.

In client language, that means:

- it is fast and browser-based
- it can work as a dashboard for merchants and staff
- it supports real-time updates through socket connections
- it has reusable modules for clients, products, sales, stock, payments, reports, storefronts, broadcasts, and automations
- it can connect to backend APIs for business data, authentication, payments, WhatsApp, Instagram, and reporting
- it is structured so new business workflows can be added without rebuilding the entire system

The architecture is organized around practical business modules: clients, products, sales, expenses, stock, reports, storefront setup, payments, WhatsApp, Instagram, automations, and admin/user management.

## Productivity Angle

Ibiza reduces repetitive work in several ways:

- bulk imports reduce manual data entry from Excel
- dashboards reduce time spent calculating performance manually
- WhatsApp broadcasts reduce one-by-one customer messaging
- automations handle reminders, replies, booking flows, and follow-ups
- reports give faster answers on sales, cash flow, profit, and expenses
- staff workflows allow correction requests instead of uncontrolled record changes
- storefront setup gives merchants a sellable online presence without hiring a full web team

The larger productivity benefit is that Ibiza moves the merchant from reactive work to proactive management. Instead of waiting for problems, the system surfaces what needs attention.

## Short Version

Ibiza is a practical business command center for African SMEs. It combines CRM, sales tracking, inventory, storefronts, payments, WhatsApp communication, social media tools, reports, and automations into one merchant-friendly platform. Its best clients are growing product and service businesses that are already selling but losing time and money because their operations are spread across too many manual tools.

# 2. Bewama

URL: https://bewama.com

## Clean Project Description

Bewama is a modern ecommerce and sourcing platform for businesses that sell industrial materials, construction supplies, chemicals, timber, silicones, adhesives, tools, and other professional-use products.

It is not just a basic online shop. The platform combines a customer-facing catalog, quote request flow, checkout experience, loyalty points, admin operations, product management, and customer engagement tools into one digital sales system.

The goal is to help a company move from manual selling through calls, WhatsApp, spreadsheets, and scattered records into a structured sales platform where customers can browse products, request quotes, place orders, earn points, receive updates, and return for future purchases.

For the client, this means fewer missed sales, faster order handling, better customer retention, and a stronger professional brand online.

## Best-Fit Clients

Bewama is ideal for businesses that sell products where customers may need either instant buying or assisted quoting.

Good-fit clients include:

- construction material suppliers
- industrial product distributors
- chemical and adhesive suppliers
- timber and hardware businesses
- B2B wholesale suppliers
- businesses serving contractors, workshops, factories, builders, or resellers
- companies currently selling through WhatsApp, calls, walk-ins, or field sales that want a more scalable digital channel

The strongest fit is a business with a real product catalog, repeat customers, bulk buyers, and operational pressure from managing orders manually.

## What Makes It Valuable

The platform supports both retail-style ecommerce and quote-based selling. This matters because industrial and B2B products are not always simple add-to-cart items. Some products can be bought directly, while others require custom pricing, bulk negotiation, delivery coordination, or staff follow-up.

The checkout experience can also support commercial nudges such as related products, points incentives, useful add-ons, reviews, referrals, subscriptions, and post-purchase prompts. This turns checkout into a sales and retention tool, not just a payment screen.

## Key Features

Buyer-side features include:

- product catalog
- category browsing
- product pages
- cart and checkout
- quote request flow
- account pages
- order history
- points hub
- resources and blog content
- customer policy pages

Admin-side features include:

- dashboard analytics
- product management
- order management
- quote management
- user management
- content management
- settings
- checkout configuration
- loyalty points configuration
- notification controls

From an operations perspective, the business can manage products, pricing, stock, quotes, customer roles, payment options, logistics settings, notification preferences, and loyalty campaigns without needing developers for every business adjustment.

## Technical Productivity

Bewama is built with Next.js, TypeScript, Supabase, Tailwind CSS, and server-side rendering patterns. Supabase supports authentication, database storage, row-level security, profiles, products, orders, quotes, settings, points, checkout configuration, and admin-controlled business rules.

The architecture is productive because the admin panel controls many business behaviors directly. Payment methods, logistics, loyalty points, checkout influence settings, post-purchase prompts, and companion product rules can be adjusted without rebuilding the platform.

In simple client language, Bewama is a digital sales engine for a serious product business. It combines ecommerce, quoting, customer retention, and admin operations into one system.

## Short Version

It supports catalog browsing, direct checkout, quote requests, loyalty points, product content, and admin operations so product businesses can move from manual selling into a structured digital sales system.

# 5. Jambo Programming Language

## Clean Project Description

Jambo is a beginner-friendly programming language designed for African communities, with syntax based on Swahili.

Instead of learning programming through English-only keywords such as `if`, `while`, and `function`, learners can write code using Swahili keywords such as `kama`, `wakati`, and `unda`.

The goal is not to replace mainstream programming languages. The goal is to make early programming education feel more accessible, culturally familiar, and inclusive for Swahili-speaking learners.

## Best-Fit Users And Communities

Jambo is especially useful for:

- African tech communities and programming education initiatives
- computer science educators in Swahili-speaking regions
- beginners and students who want to learn programming in a familiar language
- schools and bootcamps in East Africa that want more inclusive teaching tools
- open-source contributors interested in language design and cultural technology

## Technical Description

Jambo is a dynamically typed, interpreted programming language written primarily in Go.

Core components include:

- lexer and parser for tokenizing and parsing Swahili keywords into an abstract syntax tree
- interpreter for evaluating language constructs and runtime behavior
- dynamic types such as booleans, integers, floats, strings, arrays, dictionaries, and null values
- first-class functions with recursion support
- control flow through conditionals, while loops, and for-in loops
- array and dictionary operations
- user input handling through the `jaza()` function

## Technical Features

- REPL for interactive code execution
- file execution with `.jb` and `.sw` extensions
- cross-platform support for Linux, macOS, Windows, and Android through Termux
- CLI tooling built with Charm ecosystem libraries such as Bubble Tea, Glamour, and Lipgloss
- VS Code syntax highlighting extension
- documentation in English and Swahili
- automated testing and CI/CD workflows
- precompiled binaries for multiple platforms

## Technical Productivity Notes

- Written in: Go
- UI libraries: Charm Bubble Tea, Glamour, Lipgloss
- Testing: automated test suite
- Distribution: precompiled binaries
- License: GPL-2.0
- Setup: designed for fast installation and direct CLI usage
- Execution: REPL mode or script execution through the `jambo` command

## Why It Matters

Jambo is a proof-of-concept for inclusive programming education. It shows that programming concepts can be taught through local language without removing computational depth.

For learners, the value is approachability. For educators, the value is a culturally relevant tool that can reduce the first barrier to understanding programming logic.

## Short Version

Jambo is a Swahili-inspired programming language for beginner programming education. Built mainly in Go, it includes an interpreter, REPL, CLI tooling, documentation, syntax highlighting, and cross-platform support. Its purpose is to make early coding education more accessible for Swahili-speaking learners and communities.

# 6. Interview Coder

## Clean Project Description

Interview Coder is a free, open-source AI-powered desktop application for technical interview preparation.

It helps students, job seekers, bootcamp graduates, and developers practice coding problems with AI assistance. The app can help analyze a coding problem, suggest solution approaches, explain trade-offs, support debugging, and clarify time and space complexity.

The strongest positioning is as a private, customizable study tool for interview preparation. It should be presented as a learning and practice assistant, not as a tool for bypassing interview rules.

## Client-Friendly Explanation

When someone is preparing for a technical interview, they often practice coding problems from platforms such as LeetCode, HackerRank, Codewars, or custom interview sheets.

Interview Coder acts like an AI study assistant that can:

- help understand the problem statement
- suggest solution strategies
- generate sample implementations for study
- explain why an approach works
- analyze time and space complexity
- help debug errors during practice
- support learners who need affordable interview preparation

The application runs on the user's computer and can be configured with the user's own AI provider credentials.

## Best-Fit Users

Interview Coder is useful for:

- college students preparing for internships or technical interviews
- job seekers switching careers into software development
- coding bootcamp graduates who need structured practice support
- international learners who cannot afford expensive interview preparation subscriptions
- developers who want a customizable local study workflow
- companies or trainers building internal coding practice programs

## Key Advantages

- Free and open source
- Local desktop workflow
- Configurable AI provider setup
- Cross-platform architecture
- Customizable source code
- Useful for study, debugging, and explanation
- Lower cost barrier compared with paid preparation platforms

## Technical Description

Interview Coder is built with React, TypeScript, Electron, Tailwind CSS, Radix UI components, and Vite. It integrates with AI providers such as OpenAI through user-configured API keys.

Technical strengths include:

- single codebase for Windows, macOS, and Linux
- fast frontend development with Vite
- type safety through TypeScript
- desktop packaging through Electron
- modern accessible UI components through Radix UI
- modular architecture for adding or changing AI providers
- local-first application structure

## Productivity Angle

The product helps learners move faster during practice sessions by reducing the time spent stuck on unclear problem statements, syntax errors, and complexity analysis.

For developers, the open-source architecture makes it possible to modify the tool for specific languages, workflows, prompts, providers, or training environments.

## Safe Public Positioning Note

Avoid public wording that suggests stealth use during real interviews, screen-share evasion, or violating interview platform rules. The better public story is:

- private study assistant
- local coding practice tool
- AI-powered explanation and debugging support
- open-source interview preparation workflow

## Short Version

Interview Coder is an open-source AI desktop app for technical interview practice. It helps learners understand coding problems, explore solution strategies, debug practice code, and study complexity trade-offs through a configurable local workflow.
